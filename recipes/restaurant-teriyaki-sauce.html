About This Clean Teriyaki Sauce

At FitDadFeeds, I’m all about feeding my gang with real, clean ingredients—no fake stuff allowed. This Restaurant Teriyaki Sauce is a must-have for busy dads who want to level up their family’s meals without sacrificing health. As a bodybuilder and dad of six, I’ve perfected this recipe to deliver bold, restaurant-quality flavor in just 10 minutes. It’s all about real ingredients and no processed mess, so you can feel good about what you’re serving your tribe.

Why Homemade Matters

Store-bought teriyaki sauces are often packed with nasties—high-fructose corn syrup, artificial flavors, and preservatives. That’s not how I roll. Making your own clean teriyaki sauce means you control every ingredient, ensuring it’s free of the bad stuff. This recipe uses tamari or coconut aminos, organic brown sugar, and raw honey for a sauce that’s as wholesome as it is delicious. It’s quick, simple, and lets you take charge of your family’s health like a true leader.

Tips for the Cleanest Ingredients

To keep this sauce clean, check your labels. For soy sauce, choose tamari or coconut aminos with just soybeans, water, and salt—avoid brands with additives. Organic brown sugar keeps processed junk out, and raw, unfiltered honey adds natural sweetness with health benefits. I use ground ginger and garlic powder for convenience, but fresh works too if you’ve got the time. These choices align with my clean-eating ethos and make this sauce a win for your fam.

How to Use This Sauce

This Restaurant Teriyaki Sauce is versatile enough to elevate any meal. Glaze grilled salmon or chicken, use it as a marinade for steak, or toss it with stir-fried veggies. My gang goes hard for it over grilled salmon with a side of steamed broccoli—perfect for a quick family dinner. Skip the cornstarch if marinating to let the flavors soak in better. Make a double batch and store it in the fridge for up to a week to simplify meal prep. Try it with our Prosciutto Salmon with Garlic Butter for a next-level meal!

Restaurant Teriyaki Sauce

Quick Info

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Cook Time: 5 minutes

Total Time: 10 minutes

Yield: 1.5 cups

Share on Pinterest Share on Facebook Share via Text Share via Email

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (tamari or coconut aminos for a cleaner option)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 5 tbsp packed brown sugar (organic preferred)
  • 1 tbsp honey (raw, unfiltered)
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch (optional, for thickness)
  • 1/4 cup cold water

Instructions

  1. Mix soy sauce, 1 cup water, ground ginger, garlic powder, brown sugar, and honey in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir until the mixture begins to simmer.
  3. If using cornstarch, mix it with 1/4 cup cold water in a cup until dissolved.
  4. Once the sauce is simmering, whisk in the cornstarch mixture and heat until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency, about 1-2 minutes.
  5. If the sauce gets too thick, add a little more water to thin it out.

Recipe Notes

For a thicker glaze, use the cornstarch; skip it for a smoother marinade to let the flavors soak in better. Choose tamari or coconut aminos for a soy sauce with no bad stuff. Organic brown sugar avoids processed additives, and raw, unfiltered honey keeps it clean. Adjust sweetness with extra honey if your tribe likes it sweeter. This is how I roll—real ingredients only!

Substitution Tips

  • Swap tamari for coconut aminos for a soy-free option—no nasties allowed.
  • Use maple syrup instead of honey for a vegan version that’s still clean.
