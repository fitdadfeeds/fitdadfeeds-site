Restaurant Teriyaki Sauce

This clean, homemade teriyaki sauce is a game-changer for busy dads like me. No processed junk—just real ingredients that make your chicken, salmon, or veggies taste like they came from a top-notch restaurant. My squad of six devours it every time!

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 5 minutes | Total Time: 10 minutes | Yield: 1.5 cups

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (use low-sodium for a lighter option)
  • 5 tablespoons brown sugar (or coconut sugar for a cleaner swap)
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup cold water

Instructions

  1. Combine 1 cup water, soy sauce, brown sugar, honey, ginger, and garlic powder in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Mix cornstarch with 1/4 cup cold water in a small bowl until smooth.
  3. Add the cornstarch mixture to the saucepan, stirring constantly. Cook until the sauce thickens, about 2 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Use immediately or store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week.

Tips

  • Use this sauce to glaze grilled chicken, salmon, or roasted veggies for a restaurant-quality meal.
  • Double the recipe and freeze half for quick dinners.
  • Adjust honey or sugar to taste for a sweeter or less sweet sauce.
