Prosciutto Salmon with Garlic Butter

A simple yet elegant dish with crispy prosciutto and rich garlic butter, perfect for a healthy family dinner.

Categories: Dinner, Seafood

A Quick and Healthy Dinner for Busy Families

At *FitDadFeeds*, we’re passionate about creating meals that fit into the busy lives of parents while keeping health and flavor at the forefront. This *Prosciutto Salmon with Garlic Butter* is a perfect example—a wholesome dinner that comes together in under 25 minutes. With just a handful of clean ingredients, you can serve a protein-packed, low-carb meal that’s rich in healthy fats, making it ideal for keeping your family energized and happy. It’s a go-to recipe for those hectic weeknights when you want something nutritious without the hassle.

The FitDadFeeds Ethos: Clean Ingredients Matter

We believe in using whole, minimally processed ingredients to create meals that nourish without compromise. That’s why this recipe features wild-caught Alaskan sockeye salmon, which is higher in omega-3 fatty acids and lower in contaminants compared to farm-raised options. Omega-3s support heart health and reduce inflammation, making this a great choice for active families. We also use prosciutto free of nitrates and artificial preservatives—just pork and salt—to ensure a clean, savory addition to the dish. For the garlic butter, we stick to unsalted butter and fresh garlic, avoiding the soybean oil and additives often found in store-bought spreads. At *FitDadFeeds*, we encourage making your own garlic butter—it’s simple, clean, and lets you control the quality of every ingredient.

Why Wild-Caught Salmon Is Best for Your Family

Choosing wild-caught salmon isn’t just about flavor—it’s about health. Wild Alaskan sockeye salmon, like the kind we use in this recipe, is packed with omega-3s, which are essential for brain development in kids and overall wellness for adults. It’s also lower in pollutants than farm-raised salmon, aligning with our commitment to clean eating. When shopping, look for fillets that are bright pink or orange with a firm texture, and avoid any that smell fishy or look dull. If you can, opt for sustainably sourced salmon to support ethical fishing practices—a small choice that makes a big difference.

Pairing Ideas to Complete the Meal

This salmon pairs beautifully with a variety of sides, making it versatile for any family dinner. We love serving it with steamed asparagus or roasted potatoes for a hearty meal, but my family’s favorite is organic pasta—it’s a kid-friendly option that still keeps the meal clean and wholesome. For a lighter option, try a fresh green salad with olive oil and lemon dressing. If you’re meal prepping, make extra and store the leftovers in an airtight container—they’re just as delicious the next day for a quick lunch.

Prosciutto Salmon with Garlic Butter Recipe

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Cook Time: 17 minutes

Total Time: 22 minutes

Servings: 2

Print Recipe Share on Pinterest Share on Facebook Share via Text Share via Email

Ingredients

For the Salmon Filets:

  • 8 oz raw wild-caught Alaskan sockeye salmon (two 4-oz fillets)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper (optional)
  • 2 slices prosciutto (nitrate-free, just pork and salt)

For the Garlic Butter Sauce:

  • 1 1/2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Lay the 4-oz salmon fillets on a flat surface and pat dry with a paper towel. Season with the salt, pepper, onion powder, and crushed red pepper (if using).
  3. Place the salmon on a sheet tray lined with parchment paper. Lay a slice of prosciutto over each fillet—you don’t need to tuck the prosciutto under the salmon.
  4. Bake the salmon for 16–17 minutes, until it flakes easily with a fork (internal temperature of 145°F/63°C for medium). Set aside to cool slightly.
  5. During the final minutes of baking, make the garlic butter sauce. Melt the butter in a small pan over low heat, then stir in the garlic and lemon juice. Cook until the butter turns lightly golden and the garlic gives off a nutty aroma, about 1–2 minutes.
  6. Arrange the salmon on a serving dish and drizzle the garlic butter sauce over each fillet with a spoon.

Recipe Notes

This dish pairs well with many sides, including steamed asparagus, roasted potatoes, or my family’s favorite—organic pasta! For the cleanest option, choose pasta made from 100% durum wheat semolina with no additives.

Substitution Tips

If you don’t have unsalted butter, you can use ghee from a grass-fed source—just avoid store-bought spreads with seed oils or additives. For a milder flavor, reduce the garlic to 1/2 tsp and adjust the lemon juice to taste.
